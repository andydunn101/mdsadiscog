Miles - In the 1980/90's

Miles returned to recording in 1980, and continued until his death and during this period swapped labels from Columbia to Warner Brothers, which forced him to give up his publishing rights. In his was his style he was not afraid to collaborate with new artists, which included Scritti Polliti and John Lydon. During ths time he recieved the Grammy Liftime Achievment Award and the Chevalier of the Legion of Honor in Paris.

Miles passed away on September 28th, 1991 at the age of 65. His final album "Doo-Bop" was released in 1992, bringing to an end a fantasic recording legacy. My only regret about his music is that I wished I had started listing to it earlier. So my dad (as they always are) was right when he said how good jazz music is.

I don't have many of the later recordings in my CD collection, but found "Tutu" in a record shop in Manchester, and just loved the opening track:
