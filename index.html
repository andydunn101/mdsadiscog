Miles - The Greatest Jazz Musician

In 2009 I went to a Carlos Santana concert and when he appeared on stage he was wearing a T-shirt reading "Listen to Miles Davis" and I did. It must have been somewhere in my psyche as my Dad listened to jazz, so it was not great leap of faith to listen jazz.

Miles Davis (May 26, 1926 â€“ September 28, 1991) was an American jazz trumpeter, bandleader and composer. He is among the most influential and acclaimed figures in the history of jazz and 20th century music. Dring his career he took various musical directions which kept him at the forefront of most of the developments in jazz. During his time he worked with some of the greats of jazz and popular music, such as Charlie Parker, John Coltrane, Herbie Hancock and many more. In 2006, Davis was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, which recognized him as "one of the key figures in the history of jazz."

This website shows the volume of studio work that Miles undertook during his career. It does not include live albums, film scores, compilations or box sets. Watch a classic performance of "So What" below.
