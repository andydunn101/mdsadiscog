Miles - In the 1970's

The 1970's started with a new sytle of Jazz/Rock fusion with the album "Bitches Brew". The addition of guitarist in the late 60's gave this new style a more rock like feel and was promoted as such by Columbia. However, some jazz purists began to say he was "selling out". As he was appearing with rock band like The Steve Miller Band and Neil Young, he began appealing to a new audience.

During the 1970's Davis continued to record and innovate, but was now one the elder statesmen of jazz. In 1972 he was significantly hampered after a car accident in 1972 where he broke his ankles. And it was in 1975 he stopped completely to recupirate. In his biography he was open about his addictions.
