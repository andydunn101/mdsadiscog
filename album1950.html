Miles - In the 1950's

During the 50's Davis developed the cool jazz style and by the end of the decade was considered by many to be the coolest man on the planet. He was signed by the Prestige label in 1949. In 1955, he signed for Columbia records, but continued to record with Prestige as he had five albums left on his contract. It was during his period with Columbia that he was able to put togther one of his most prestigous bands consisting of saxophonist John Coltrane, pianist Red Garland, bassist Paul Chambers, and drummer Philly Joe Jones.

During this time the members of his band changed regularly and different musician, like Cannonball Adderley, played in Miles band. However it was with Columbia the he made some of his most famous recordings, culminating in "Kind of Blue" in 1959. Kind of Blue became a landmark album in modern jazz and probably the most popular album of his career
