Miles - In the 1960's

The 1960's opened with the "Sketches of Spain" album which was a fusion of jazz, Classical and world music. During the early 60's John Coltrane left to pursue a solo career, but appeared on European tours and on few tracks of the album "Some Day My Prince Will Come". It was during this period that the second great quintet was formed in 1964 and laster unti 1968.

The quintet consisted of Davis, Wayne Shorter on saxophone, Ron Carter on Bass, Herbie Hanconk on piano and Tony Williams on drums. During this period they started experimenting more with electonic instruments. With the ablbums "Miles in the Sky" and "Filles de Kilimanjaro" using electric bass, bass and piano. The decade ended with the album "In a Silent Way" which returned him to the top of the charts and a Grammy nomimation.
